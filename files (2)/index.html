What Is This

The Lollie Road Data Center Deal

The City of Conway quietly negotiated a deal with Forgelight Ventures, LLC to build a $1 billion, 300,000 sq ft AI data center on Arkansas River bottomland along Lollie Road. The city agreed to a 65% property tax abatement for 30 years. Approximately 160 acres of agricultural land were rezoned from A-1 to I-3 (Heavy Industrial) in April 2025. When asked about NDAs, the city said it had "no records responsive" — yet the MOU released in this same FOIA batch was stamped "CONFIDENTIAL, TRADE SECRET INFORMATION, NOT SUBJECT TO DISCLOSURE REQUESTS." Portions of the rezoned land lie within the 100-year floodplain. The city has zero environmental review documents on file.

Key Findings

What the Documents Show

01

The MOU Was Stamped "Not Subject to Disclosure" — Then Disclosed

The Forgelight Ventures MOU bears the header "CONFIDENTIAL, TRADE SECRET INFORMATION, NOT SUBJECT TO DISCLOSURE REQUESTS" on every page. Yet the city released it under FOIA. Simultaneously, when asked for NDA/confidentiality agreements, the city said it had "no records responsive." The contradiction is now documented.

Source: Forgelight Ventures LLC_MOU.pdf · LET_RESPONSE_2026-76.pdf
02

65% Tax Abatement for 30 Years — Up to $50 Billion in Personal Property

The MOU commits Conway to a net 65% property tax abatement for 30 years. Bond structures cap real property at $10 billion and personal property at $50 billion. Conway Corp will provide cooling water via treated effluent from the Tupelo Bayou Wastewater Treatment Plant, with special franchise fee arrangements separate from what residents pay.

Source: Forgelight Ventures LLC_MOU.pdf
03

The Rezoning Applicant Was the Conway Development Corporation — Not the Developer

The rezoning application was filed by Jenifer Kendrick of the Conway Development Corporation (Chamber of Commerce) on behalf of landowner Johnny Adams. The actual data center developer, Forgelight Ventures, is not named in the rezoning application. The land was rezoned from Agricultural (A-1) to Heavy Industrial (I-3).

Source: Rezoning Cover Letter.Adams.4.3.25_v1.pdf · REZ-0425-0052_160 Acres Lollie Rd A-1 to I-3_RR.docx
04

Portions of the Property Are in the 100-Year Floodplain. No Environmental Review Was Done.

The city's own planning staff memo notes: "Portions of the property lie within the 100-year floodplain." No environmental assessment was conducted. The FOIA response for environmental review documents returned: "The City of Conway has no records responsive to this item." — for ADEE, Dept of Health, Army Corps of Engineers, and EPA.

Source: REZ-0425-0052_160 Acres Lollie Rd A-1 to I-3_RR.docx · LET_RESPONSE_2026-76.pdf
05

Water Concerns Are Documented — Alderman Acknowledged Them

The FOIA release includes 19+ emails from residents titled "Concerned Lollie Rd Resident," plus multiple threads explicitly about water usage. One resident cited news reports about neighborhoods losing water near Meta data centers. Alderman Drew Spurgers replied: "Understood on the concerns. I'll visit with Bret Carroll on this and see if there's more we need to stipulate as we move forward."

Source: Data Center.pdf · Multiple .msg files in FOIA release
06

City Passed Data Center Zoning Laws in 2023 — Well Before This Became Public

Conway passed O-23-55 (noise rules for data centers) in July 2023 via special meeting, and O-23-72 (zoning definitions) in fall 2023. These established the legal framework before the Lollie Road rezoning application was filed in April 2025. The MOU with Forgelight appears to have been negotiated in the same period.

Source: O-23-55 ORD_Adm_Data Center Crypto Noise Ordinance.docx · O-23-72_ORD_Planning_Data Centers.docx
07

~50 Jobs. $1 Billion Investment. $20 Million Per Job.

The MOU states the project creates "approximately 50 new high-quality jobs." At $1 billion in investment, that's approximately $20 million per job — in a deal supported by a 65% tax abatement over 30 years. Whether this trade-off serves Conway residents is a legitimate public question that deserves an open debate.

Source: Forgelight Ventures LLC_MOU.pdf — Note: jobs/investment ratio analysis is TDS campaign commentary, not a document claim.
Chronology

Timeline of Events

July 25, 2023
O-23-55 passed via special meeting — noise ordinance for data centers, in response to Arkansas Act 851 (Arkansas Data Centers Act). Legal groundwork begins.
September – November 2023
O-23-72 adopted — Conway Zoning Code amended to formally define "Data Center (Limited)" and "Data Center (General)" use classifications.
Date Unknown (Pre-2025)
Forgelight Ventures MOU signed — City commits to 65% tax abatement / 30 years, cooling water from Tupelo Bayou WWTP. Stamped "Confidential, Not Subject to Disclosure."
March 2025
Annexation applications filed (ANN-0325-0046 and ANN-0325-0047) — adjacent properties brought into city limits, noted in planning documents as precursor to the rezoning.
April 3, 2025
Rezoning application filed — Jenifer Kendrick (Conway Development Corp) files REZ-0425-0052 on behalf of landowner Johnny Adams. 160± acres, A-1 to I-3. Explicitly states purpose: "to host a data center campus."
April 14, 2025
Planning Commission meeting — public comments submitted. Internal Teams chats and emails show active discussion among planning staff about the rezoning packet.
April 22, 2025
City Council agenda includes rezoning — April 22 City Council meeting includes the Lollie Road rezoning item.
May 2025
Planning Commission Report issued — formal 20MB+ documentation package on the rezoning recommendation.
June 24, 2025
City Council Meeting Minutes — rezoning-related items formalized in official record.
July – August 2025
Resident concerns escalate — multiple Lollie Road residents email aldermen. Alderman Drew Spurgers responds to one, acknowledging water concerns and promising follow-up with "Bret Carroll."
November 25, 2025
O-25-89 passed — Conway Zoning Code amended adding Article X on Development Review. Signed by Mayor Bart Castleberry.
Early 2026
FOIA-2026-76 filed by Matthew Mattingly requesting all data center-related records. Response received April 8, 2026 — 169 documents released.
May 4, 2026
This site goes live. Documents published in full. Toads Don't Suck campaign continues. 🐸
