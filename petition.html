To: Mayor Bart Castleberry & the Conway City Council

We, the undersigned residents and supporters of Conway, Arkansas, call on the City of Conway to immediately halt any further action on the proposed data center development along the Lollie Road corridor and the Arkansas River until the following conditions are met.

In April 2025, the City of Conway approved the rezoning of 455 acres of land along Lollie Road to I-3 Heavy Industrial classification — the most intensive industrial zoning available — in apparent anticipation of a major data center development deal. This rezoning was executed under a nondisclosure agreement that prevented Conway residents from having any meaningful input into a decision that will significantly affect our community for generations.

Data centers are not passive neighbors. Large-scale facilities of this type consume millions of gallons of water daily for cooling systems, generate substantial heat output, require massive electrical infrastructure, and produce persistent noise. Sited on the banks of the Arkansas River — the same river our community celebrates every year at Toad Suck Daze — these impacts represent a direct threat to our water quality, wildlife habitat, and quality of life.

This concern is not abstract. Conway Corporation issued a voluntary water restriction notice this week due to below-average rainfall across the region. While that restriction is unrelated to the proposed data center, it raises a question Conway residents deserve an honest answer to: if our water supply is already under strain from natural conditions, what happens to availability and cost when a facility consuming millions of gallons daily comes online next to our river? This is a speculative concern — but it is a reasonable one, and it deserves a public answer before any ground is broken.

We are not opposed to economic development. We are opposed to decisions of this magnitude being made behind closed doors, under nondisclosure agreements, without the knowledge or consent of the people of Conway.

We Demand the Following:

1
A full public hearing before any development agreements are finalized, ground is broken, or permits are issued for any data center or heavy industrial facility along the Lollie Road / Arkansas River corridor.
2
Full disclosure of all agreements, letters of intent, NDAs, and communications between the City of Conway and any data center developer — in full compliance with the Arkansas Freedom of Information Act.
3
An independent environmental impact assessment specifically addressing water usage, river quality, air quality, and ecological impact on the Arkansas River ecosystem.
4
A community vote or council resolution that formally acknowledges the public's right to weigh in on any I-3 Heavy Industrial development adjacent to the Arkansas River.

The Arkansas River is not a resource to be quietly handed over. It belongs to all of us — the toads, the families, the fishermen, the festival-goers, and the generations of Conway residents yet to come.
