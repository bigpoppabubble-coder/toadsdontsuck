// What's happening

The Issue

Conway, Arkansas is home to one of the most beloved regional festivals in the South — Toad Suck Daze, a celebration rooted in the history and character of our Arkansas River community. The Toad Suck Lock and Dam isn't just a landmark. It's our identity.

In April 2025, the City of Conway quietly rezoned 455 acres of land along the Lollie Road corridor — riverfront land, near that very dam — to I-3 Heavy Industrial classification. The rezoning was approved by the Planning Commission with minimal public attention.

What we now believe: this rezoning was done in advance of a deal — signed under a nondisclosure agreement — to build a massive data center on the banks of the Arkansas River.

Data centers are not benign neighbors. They consume millions of gallons of water daily for cooling. They generate significant heat and require enormous electrical infrastructure. Sited next to a river, these impacts become direct threats to the ecosystem, water quality, and the community that depends on both.

This concern is not abstract. Conway Corporation issued a voluntary water restriction notice this week due to below-average rainfall across the region. While that restriction is unrelated to the proposed data center, it raises a question Conway residents deserve an honest answer to: if our water supply is already under strain from natural conditions, what happens to availability and cost when a facility consuming millions of gallons daily comes online next to our river? This is a speculative concern — but it is a reasonable one, and it deserves a public answer before any ground is broken.

Conway residents were given no voice. No vote. No notice. That ends now.

// The paper trail

The Evidence

This isn't speculation. It's public record — buried in a city report most people never read.

Planning Commission

The Rezoning

In April 2025, the Conway Planning Commission approved the rezoning of approximately 455 acres along the Lollie Road corridor to I-3 Heavy Industrial zoning — the most intensive industrial classification available. This is documented in the City of Conway's own 2025 State of the City report.

NDA / Secrecy

The Secret Deal

A development agreement — believed to involve a major data center operator — was signed by city officials under a nondisclosure agreement. Conway residents were not informed. Arkansas FOIA law places strict limits on the use of NDAs to conceal information affecting public health and land use.

FOIA Filed

We Asked. Officially.

A formal Freedom of Information Act request has been submitted to the City of Conway demanding all records related to the development agreement, the NDA, the rezoning communications, and any environmental review documents. The city has 3 business days to respond under Arkansas law.

// Who we are

About This Campaign

Toads Don't Suck is a grassroots community campaign started by Conway residents who believe that major decisions about our city — especially decisions that affect our river, our environment, and our future — should be made in public, with public input, not behind closed doors under nondisclosure agreements.

"They'd suck until they puffed up like toads." Conway built a whole festival on that story. We're not going to let the same thing happen to our river.

We are not anti-business. We are not anti-development. We are pro-transparency, pro-community, and pro-river. If a data center is coming to Conway, the people of Conway deserve to know what it means for their water, their air, their land, and their quality of life — before the concrete is poured.

This campaign was built from scratch by community members with no political backing and no outside funding. We believe in the power of an informed, organized community to hold its local government accountable.
